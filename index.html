Dip Powder Nails

Dip powder nails are a popular and versatile option. This process involves applying a base coat, dipping the nails into a pigmented powder, and then sealing with a top coat. This method offers a durable and long-lasting finish without the use of UV light. Dip powder nails are known for their strength and resistance to chipping, making them an excellent choice for those who want low-maintenance, vibrant nails.

Acrylic Nails

Acrylic nails are a classic choice for those seeking strength and durability. This process involves using a liquid monomer and a powder polymer to create a strong protective layer over the natural nails. Acrylic nails are customizable, allowing for varied shapes, lengths, and designs. They are particularly resilient and can be filled as the natural nail grows, making them a practical option for long-term wear.

Gel Nails

Gel nails are a popular option for a natural and glossy finish. This process involves applying layers of gel polish onto the natural nails, with each layer being cured under an LED or UV light. Gel nails are known for their flexibility, giving a natural feel while providing long-lasting color and shine. They can be easily soaked off and reapplied, making them a great choice for those who prefer to change their nail color frequently.

Sgel (Soft Gel) Nails

Sgel, also known as "soft gel," is a revolutionary nail extension system that combines the flexibility and lightweight nature of gel with the durability of acrylic. This innovative approach provides clients with the best of both worlds—nails that are strong, flexible, and natural-looking. Sgel nails are formulated using a soft gel material that offers a natural feel, making it a comfortable option for extended wear.
